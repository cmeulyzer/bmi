BMI Definition

According to wikipedia the Body Mass Index (BMI) was designed to be used as a simple means of classifying average sedentary (physically inactive) populations, with an average body composition.

Formula

World Health Organization (WHO) recommendations

Category BMI kg/m 2
Very severely underweight <15
Severely underweight 15 - 16
Underweight 16 - 18.5
Normal (healthy weight) 18.5 - 25
Overweight 25 - 30
Obese Class I (Moderately obese) 30 - 35
Obese Class II (Severely obese) 35 - 40
Obese Class III (Very severely obese) >=40

Limitations

  • BMI exaggerates thinness in short people and fatness in tall people
  • Does not differentiate between muscle mass and fat mass
  • The are questions about the relationship between the BMI and health
